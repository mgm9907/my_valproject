1. What are the different services under Portfolio Management Service and which ones do we offer?

There are 3 types of services under Portfolio Management Service (PMS) namely -
1. Discretionary Portfolio Management Service – The client invests the funds with the PMS and the PMS has full discretion to invest in the securities of its choice. The same is done through the client executing a power of attorney (POA) in favour of the PMS.
2. Advisory Portfolio Management Service – The client takes advise from the PMS on the securities to buy/sell, but the final decision and control to execute the same lies with the client.
3. Non-Discretionary Portfolio Management Service – In this case, the PMS merely carries out the operational aspects including buying, selling and settlement on behalf of the client based on the instructions of the client. The discretion, in this case, lies with the client.

2. What is the difference between a Discretionary Portfolio Management Service and Mutual Fund?


Portfolio Management Service (PMS) runs strategies and invests client’s funds into portfolios created under these strategies. The securities bought in the portfolio strategies are in the name of the clients under the PMS.
In case of Mutual Funds, the client’s funds are invested in the schemes of the Mutual Funds and clients are issued units for the same. The securities bought in the portfolios of the schemes are in the name of the Mutual Funds.
In the case of PMS, the minimum amount of investment is Rs.25 lakhs (will go to Rs.50 lakhs as per the SEBI press release https://www.sebi.gov.in/media/press-releases/nov-2019/sebi-board-meeting_45022.htmldated 20th November 2019) as initial investment whereas in the case of Mutual Fund schemes, the minimum amount of investment can be as low as Rs. 500 in the case of SIPs and Rs.5000 in the case of lumpsum investment.

3. Who can invest with Valcreate PMS?

We cater to all segments of investors such as individuals, family offices, HUFs, corporates, NRIs and any other non-individual entities allowed to invest in PMS as long as they meet the required KYC criteria.

4. Can I start with SIP in PMS?

The minimum initial investment of Rs.25 lakhs (will go to Rs.50 lakhs as per the SEBI press release https://www.sebi.gov.in/media/press-releases/nov-2019/sebi-board-meeting_45022.html dated 20th November 2019) needs to be done first, post which subjects to the condition that the market value of the investment remains above Rs.25 lakhs (will go to Rs.50 lakhs as per the SEBI press release https://www.sebi.gov.in/media/press-releases/nov-2019/sebi-board-meeting_45022.html dated 20th November 2019), a client can invest any amount whether in the form of a SIP or any other values at any point.

5. If I have total savings of Rs.10 crs, can I give them to Valcreate to manage and create wealth?

Depending on your risk profile and objectives, we would recommend and allocate your funds into low risk to high-risk securities. The choice of investment portfolio and strategies would be commensurate with the above.

6. Once you invest with Valcreate PMS, will we invest all the money immediately?

It is a difficult call to time security prices and markets and may depend upon the situation and the overall environment. More often than not, we would refrain from taking cash calls but in some situations, or at some points in time, if there is a significant overvaluation in the market, we may take longer than usual to invest than otherwise.

7. How is the taxation of my investments in Valcreate PMS strategies?

It is the same as if you were an individual tax filer. Thus, Valcreate does not pay taxes on behalf of its investors and sends the investors a P&L statement for the same at the end of each period, which they can use to manage their tax filing.

8. What should be the time horizon for my investment with Valcreate PMS to get reasonable returns?

Since securities markets are unpredictable, we do not describe a timeframe to generate reasonable returns. However, we are long term investors and we prefer that our clients stay with us for as long as possible to reach their wealth maximization goals. As a rule of thumb, we advise reviewing portfolio returns over 3 to 5 years timeframe from the initial investment date.

9. I have securities worth Rs. 25 lakhs or more, can I bring them to Valcreate PMS to manage?

Yes, you can but you will have to transfer your securities in the existing demat account to a new demat account under Valcreate PMS. This amount will go to Rs.50 lakhs as per the SEBI press release https://www.sebi.gov.in/media/press-releases/nov-2019/sebi-board-meeting_45022.html dated 20th November 2019.

10. Do you invest in derivatives such as futures, options of indices and stocks?

Currently we are not taking any exposure to any derivatives.
